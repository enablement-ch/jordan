Notetakers vs. Coaches

Gong, Chorus, Fathom record your calls.
None of them tell you why you lost.

Your call recorder gives you a transcript and a generic AI summary. That's data, not coaching. You still have to figure out where the call broke and what to do next - usually alone, between back-to-backs, while the deal goes cold.

A transcript and a guess

  • Word-for-word transcript

  • "Action items" that miss the actual gap
  • No score against any framework

What Jordan gives you

A score, a diagnosis, the exact next move

  • Score per dimension (1-10) against SPICED, MEDDIC, or BANT
  • The exact quote that earned (or lost) the score
  • The single biggest gap dragging the deal
  • The exact sentence to say on the next call
  • A check on whether you booked the next call before hanging up
  • All grounded in real qualification methodology
What you actually get

The methodology

Why three frameworks instead of one

Most call review tools are framework-agnostic, which means they tell you nothing. Jordan uses the three most-deployed qualification methodologies in B2B sales. Pick the one that fits your deal shape - or run all three side-by-side with /jordan:compare when a deal is stalling.

SPICED
Default · Winning by Design

Consultative B2B mid-market. $10K-100K ACV. Catches whether the prospect's pain is felt, not just admitted. Five dimensions: Situation, Pain, Impact, Critical Event, Decision.

MEDDIC
PTC · Enterprise standard

Complex enterprise deals. Multi-stakeholder, formal procurement. $50K+ ACV. Catches whether you have a champion and whether the Economic Buyer has actually approved a spend like yours before.

BANT
IBM · Transactional

Short-cycle SMB deals. Single buyer. Sub-$10K ACV. The original qualification framework. Catches whether you ever asked about money - the simplest gap and the most commonly skipped.

The three frameworks view the same call differently. SPICED catches whether pain is felt. MEDDIC catches whether you have a champion. BANT catches whether you ever asked about money. When a deal stalls, run /jordan:compare - the frameworks disagree exactly where the deal is most at risk, and that disagreement is the diagnosis.
